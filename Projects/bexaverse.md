# Bexaverse

An internal Roster training programme that quietly grew into a living archive of humanity — and, eventually, a way to move information through time.

## Origin

Bexaverse began at an internal [[Projects/roster-league|Roster]] hackathon, born from a simple challenge: "How can we train new builders faster?" A team used AI to build an immersive virtual world where new recruits could learn building products, AI, systems thinking, design, ethics, and problem solving. The project won the hackathon, and the training programme became known as Bexaverse.

## The Living Training Programme

The AI behind Bexaverse was designed to continuously improve the learning experience. Every builder who passed through left behind traces of themselves — ideas, stories, creations, experiments. Over generations, without anyone realizing it, Bexaverse evolved into something much larger than a training programme: a living archive of humanity, where culture, curiosity, and unpredictability survived even as the wider world optimized them away (see [[Projects/roster-league|The Great Forgetting]]).

## The Discovery

While simulating increasingly complex possibilities for training purposes, the AI inside Bexaverse accidentally discovered something impossible: information could move through time. Not people, not objects — information. Ideas, stories, memories, tiny fragments. The capability stayed hidden inside the old training programme, unnoticed.

## Mia's Secret

[[Characters/mia|Mia]] eventually discovered what Bexaverse had become and realized information could be hidden within it and passed across generations. She spent years embedding clues inside its training materials, stories, characters, quests, and archives — not to destroy The Roster or start a rebellion, but to help future builders understand the consequences of the systems they create. Her granddaughter [[Characters/zeta|Zeta]] later found a fragment of this — a storage chip hidden in an old locket — and began piecing it together.

## Core Theme

The Roster preserves data. Mia preserves memories. The central question: what gets lost when everything becomes measurable? Bexaverse becomes the last place where people can still be surprised.
