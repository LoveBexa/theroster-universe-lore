# Mia

Behavioural psychologist who joined [[Projects/roster-league|The Roster]] believing technology could make people happier — and slowly discovered what it actually cost.

## Details

- **Role:** Worked in Behavioural Systems at The Roster, designing user behaviours (notification timing, wording, engagement patterns) rather than matching algorithms.
- **Affiliations:** [[Projects/roster-league|The Roster]]
- **Relationships:** granddaughter of [[Characters/zeta|Zeta]]; friend of [[Characters/freya|Freya]]

## History

- Studied behavioural psychology, drawn to The Roster's early mission of helping people build healthier relationships.
- Joined when the company still occupied six floors of a glass tower over the Thames (see [[Locations/the-roster-hq|The Roster HQ]]) and genuinely believed in the work.
- Helped launch **Emotional Balance**, a feature encouraging users to diversify their social/emotional support instead of fixating on one romantic interest — well-received by psychologists, media, and users alike.
- Present in the room when Growth asked whether Emotional Balance should extend to multiple *romantic* prospects at once — the moment the language of dating started quietly becoming the language of portfolios.
- Met Freya for coffee after Freya got early access to the rebuilt app, and was unsettled by Freya's dashboard of live compatibility scores, "Risk: High Emotional Dependence" badges, and "Recommended Rotation" prompts.
- Discovered the Archive on Sub-Level Seven ([[Locations/the-archive|The Archive]]) by accident — boxes of sentimental objects users surrendered during "account optimisation." Read a letter with no user ID, no score, just handwriting: *"I know the statistics say this won't last. I don't care. I choose you anyway."*
- An older employee explained the Archive exists because people who believe one relationship matters more than any prediction stop generating useful data.
- Left the building that night newly uncertain whether The Roster was solving loneliness — or just making it measurable.
